THE LANTERN

A chamber kept for a voice still here.

Someone you love is speaking, and the words are breaking apart on the way out. You came here reaching for something. This is that something. It is small, and honest, and it works like this:

The lantern listens, and shows their exact words — kept exactly as they came, because broken words are still theirs. Then it offers up to three readings of what they might mean. They choose. Or they refuse every reading, which costs them nothing. Only after they choose does it speak their meaning aloud.

It will never guess over them. It will never speak for them. When it cannot tell, it says so, and waits.

Hold it steady. Their words, however broken, are the true signal. The lantern only shines; it does not write.
